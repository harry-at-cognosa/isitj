Class Action Lawsuit Against Chobani Claims Yogurt Includes Toxic Chemicals

So much for "only natural ingredients."

By Amanda Mactas · PUBLISHED: DEC 02, 2025 5:25 PM EST

Eugene Gologursky — Getty Images

https://www.delish.com/food-news/a69597608/class-action-lawsuit-against-chobani/
  • A class action lawsuit has been filed against Chobani for false advertising.
  • The lawsuit claims that testing shows the presence of toxic chemicals in its yogurt, while the company advertises that its products contain only natural ingredients.
  • The chemicals found in the yogurt are known to cause adverse health effects.

Chobani is the latest food brand under fire after a class-action lawsuit. The lawsuit, which was brought by a California resident who purchased the brand's nonfat plain Greek yogurt in 2023, alleges that the company has misled consumers through false advertising. According to the suit, third-party testing found man-made plastic chemicals in the brand's products, while the company advertises its yogurt as containing "only natural ingredients."

The testing, conducted by PlasticList, found that Chobani yogurt contained four types of phthalates—a chemical used in plastics known to have adverse health effects, including negative impacts on reproductive health, disruption of the endocrine system, and even the potential to cause cancer. The testing suggests that the presence of these chemicals is likely due to the yogurt's plastic containers, which leach into the product over time.

The plaintiff claims she would not have purchased the yogurt if she had known it contained phthalates. The lawsuit seeks to represent all U.S. residents who have purchased either Chobani nonfat plain Greek yogurt or Chobani whole milk plain Greek yogurt, along with similar products, within the statute of limitations.

However, when asked to comment on the litigation, the company stated, "Chobani has moved to dismiss the allegations raised by plaintiff Wysocki as they lack merit. In a hearing held in August, the court indicated it will dismiss the case in a formal order. As we have always maintained, our products include only natural ingredients. We are looking forward to the court's decision."

While the lawsuit only pertains to Chobani, unfortunately, other very popular brands were found to contain phthalates through PlasticList's testing. You may want to do a deep dive into your favorite products from Ghirardelli, Coca-Cola, Starbucks, and Gerber, too (just to name a few).
